    <p class="eyebrow">Where we come from</p>
    <h2>Additional work from our university origins</h2>
    <div class="prose" style="max-width:72ch">
      <p>Before LiDAR Matrix, our team developed its roadside-LiDAR methods through applied research at the University of Nevada, Reno. That body of work includes the first LiDAR-automated pedestrian warning beacon (RRFB); a real-time turning-movement and pedestrian-count dashboard for RTC of Washoe County; statewide, data-driven deployment recommendations for pedestrian hybrid beacons (PHBs) and midblock pedestrian signals (MPSs) for Nevada DOT; and independent third-party evaluation of an AI-powered pedestrian-actuation system under a USDOT SMART grant.</p>
      <p>The team also supported Safe Streets and Roads for All data collection in Greeley, CO; conducted a <a href="https://assets.rtcwashoe.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/19182804/City-of-Reno-Micromobility-Pilot-Project_UNR-LiDAR-Study.pdf">City of Reno micromobility pilot before-and-after study</a>; evaluated the <a href="https://assets.rtcwashoe.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/19182806/Midtown-Post-Condition-Traffic-Study.pdf">Virginia Street Bus RAPID Transit (BRT) extension in Midtown Reno</a>; and collected roughly 20 sites of multimodal count, speed, and near-miss data for Nevada DOT's Safety Management Plans on <a href="https://www.dot.nv.gov/home/showpublisheddocument/23914/638926684655200000">Flamingo Road (Las Vegas)</a> and <a href="https://www.dot.nv.gov/home/showpublisheddocument/23916/638926684965770000">South Virginia Street (Reno)</a>.</p>
      <p>For the Nevada Office of Traffic Safety, the team measured vehicle speeds at 10 schools in Clark County and 5 in Washoe County, focusing on whether drivers reduce speed entering school zones with flashing versus passive signage, alongside multimodal counts and near misses, to inform proposed legislation for automated speed-camera enforcement in school zones.</p>
